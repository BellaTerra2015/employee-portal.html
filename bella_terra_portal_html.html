About This Manual & Company Overview
📘 Read — No initials required
▼

While Bella Terra was established as, and is registered as, a limited liability corporation, we use the terms "Bella Terra, LLC," "Bella Terra," and "the Company" interchangeably throughout this manual.

Employees are encouraged to ask questions and provide feedback to their Team Leader, Foreman, Supervisor, or Management. Please bring any errors or suggestions to the attention of Heather Hooper.

Purpose

This manual is designed to develop clear communication of company policies and provide a positive contribution to team morale and operational success. Each team member has a direct impact on the Company's ongoing success.

Operations, Scheduling & Payroll
✍ Initial required after reading
▼

4/10 Work Week

Bella Terra operates on a 4/10 schedule — four 10-hour days per week. This agreement is reviewed and re-signed annually.

Rest and Meal Breaks

  • Paid 10-minute rest break for every 4 hours worked
  • Unpaid 30-minute meal break when working more than 5 hours
  • Breaks may not be skipped or waived to leave early
  • Notify your supervisor immediately if you are unable to take a break

Payroll

  • Time cards must be accurately completed each day
  • Overtime is calculated on hours worked over 40 per week
  • Direct deposit is available and encouraged

Employee Health & Welfare / Paid Sick Leave
📘 Read — No initials required
▼

Paid Sick Leave

Employees earn 1 hour of paid sick leave for every 40 hours worked. Sick leave may be used beginning on your 90th calendar day of employment.

Authorized Uses

  • Your own or a family member's illness, injury, or health condition
  • Preventive medical care
  • School or workplace closure due to a public health order
  • Issues related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

Employer-Funded Medical Care

Bella Terra covers up to 4 visits per year at Vital Care Primary & Urgent Care, 1207 SE Rasmussen Blvd, Suite 119, Battle Ground, WA · (360) 723-0528

Questions? Contact Liz Prather at office [at] 1BellaTerra [dot] com or call L&I at 1-866-219-7321.

Drug & Alcohol Policy
✍ Initial required after reading
▼

Zero Tolerance Policy

Bella Terra has a zero-tolerance policy for illegal substances, misuse of prescription medications, and alcohol use during work hours.

Marijuana — Washington State Notice

While recreational marijuana use is legal in Washington State for adults off-duty, Bella Terra's operations involve safety-sensitive positions. Any detectable level of marijuana impairment during work hours is prohibited. Pre-employment, reasonable suspicion, and post-accident testing for marijuana are in effect.

Consequences

Violations are subject to disciplinary action up to and including immediate termination.

Initial to confirm: I have read and understand Bella Terra's Drug & Alcohol Policy including marijuana provisions.

Out-of-Town Project Housing Policy
✍ Initial required after reading
▼

Company-Provided Housing

When Bella Terra assigns employees to out-of-town projects requiring four (4) or more employees, the Company may arrange lodging in a rented home ("company housing").

Strictly Prohibited in Company Housing

  • Alcohol in any form
  • Illegal drugs or drug paraphernalia
  • Marijuana in any form (including legal recreational use)
  • Vaping, e-cigarettes, or tobacco/nicotine products used indoors
  • Guests not employed by Bella Terra on the same project

Room Assignments & Privacy

Any employee sharing housing with employees of a different gender will be assigned a private bedroom with a private or en-suite bathroom. This policy exists to protect the safety, dignity, and privacy of all employees regardless of gender.

Non-Solicitation of Customers

For 18 months following separation from Bella Terra, you agree not to directly solicit any current Bella Terra customer. This does NOT prevent you from working in the landscaping or hydroseeding industry for another employer.
